Data Stories

With the expansion of artificial intelligence and machine learning applications into every sphere of life, the search for quality data has also increased. From being the target for “untapped” data mining, to being a site for data collection and a contributor to the bulk of data labelling (ghost work) labour, the Continent plays a central role in the global data landscape. Yet, the continent’s relationship with the rest of the global academic and research landscape is often marked with unparalleled power asymmetries, deficit narratives, and complex and nuanced systematic historical exploitations. Data practices are barely critically examined, simplistic approaches that reduce the continent's complex challenges to data and AI permeate and furthermore, African stories are often narrated by non-Africans. Underpinned by nuances and critical approaches to data practices, the Data Economies working group envisions research that fundamentally centres Africa and Africans.
